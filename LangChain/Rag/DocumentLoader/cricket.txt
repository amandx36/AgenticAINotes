Here's a poem on Cricket:
The Gentleman's Game
Under the blazing sun or floodlit skies,
Twenty-two warriors in whites or colored guise,
The pitch whispers secrets, the crowd holds its breath,
As leather meets willow in a dance with death.
The bowler charges like a storm untamed,
Arm swinging lightning, the ball fiercely aimed.
It swings, it seams, it spins with cunning art,
To outwit the batman who stands with a brave heart.
The batsman waits, eyes sharp as a hawk,
Feet dancing lightly on the crease's chalk.
A cover drive flows like a river serene,
Or a mighty six that paints the sky green.
Boundaries roar with thunderous delight,
Screams of "Howzat!" echo through the night.
Wickets tumble like castles in sand,
Heroes rise and fall by the captain's command.
From dusty maidans to Lord's hallowed ground,
Legends are born where the willow makes sound.
Sachin, Kohli, or a kid in the street,
Cricket binds nations in rhythm and beat.
It's more than a game, it's a fever, a fire,
A saga of passion, of grit and desire.
In every heartbeat, in every cheer,
Cricket lives on, year after glorious year.
